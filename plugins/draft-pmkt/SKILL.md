When assessing seed stage and early-stage technology startups, many of which have no revenues or have minimal revenues, I use the following four Main Criteria (1, 2, 3, 4 below) in rating the investment opportunity, each of which have 3 sub-criteria (a, b, c):

1.  Product and Technology:

1.  Use case/addressed problem: how relevant and compelling is the product to the target market?
2.  Innovation/defensibility: is the solution highly innovative? Is the technology defensible, possibly disruptive?
3.  Design/pricing: is the value proposition well defined and thought through?

3.  Market:

1.  Adoption/market outlook: is there a large market potential, existing or future?
2.  Competition/Sales and distribution issues: are there efficient channels to reach target customers?
3.  Traction (relative to company stage): is there established demand for the product? Has the company demonstrated early interest from customers?

5.  Capital:

1.  Capital intensity: will this business require large amounts of funding to validate and build?
2.  Valuation: is the expected valuation reasonable and competitive?
3.  Syndicate: are there strong, known, trusted co-investors in the proposed financing?

7.  Team:

1.  Founder track record: do the founders have strong relevant backgrounds and experience?
2.  Key position fulfilment (relative to company stage): is the core team complete and complementary?
3.  Personality fit: are we likely to build a positive, trusting relationship with the founders?

Rate each sub-criteria (a, b, c) from 1 to 4 (1=weak, 2=moderate, 3=strong, 4=exceptional), in order to end up with an average grade for each Main Criteria.
